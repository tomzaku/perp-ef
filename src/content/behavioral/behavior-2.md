The interviewer is testing whether you've done your homework and whether your motivations align with the team's work. Generic answers are a red flag.

## Examples

**Input:** "Why do you want to work at [Company]?"
**Output:** A response that connects specific company attributes to your personal goals and experience.

## Solution

```text
Framework: Research → Connection → Contribution

1. SPECIFIC OBSERVATION: "I've been following [Company]'s work on
   [specific project/product]. The way you approached [technical
   challenge] with [specific technology/approach] stood out to me."

2. PERSONAL CONNECTION: "That resonates with me because in my
   current role, I've been focused on [related area]. For example,
   I [specific achievement that connects]."

3. FUTURE CONTRIBUTION: "I'd love to bring my experience with
   [relevant skill] to help [specific team/project] tackle
   [challenge you've identified]. I see a real opportunity to
   [specific value you'd add]."
```

## Explanation

**What interviewers look for:**
- Evidence you researched the company beyond the careers page
- Alignment between your interests and the team's actual work
- Enthusiasm that feels authentic, not performative

**Research sources:**
- Company engineering blog and tech talks
- Recent product launches or open-source contributions
- Team members' conference talks or blog posts
- Job description specifics (technologies, challenges mentioned)

**Avoid:**
- "I heard the pay is great" — never lead with compensation
- "I want to learn" — show what you bring, not just what you take
- Generic praise ("You're the best company") — be specific
- Mentioning perks (free food, office) — focus on the work
