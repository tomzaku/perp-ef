# Informed Consent Form

## Research Title

University Students' Perceptions of Using Generative AI in Academic English Writing: Usage, Benefits, and Plagiarism Concerns — A Case Study from a Vietnamese University

---

## Dear Participant,

You are invited to participate in a research study investigating university students' perceptions of using Generative AI tools (such as ChatGPT, Google Gemini, Microsoft Copilot) in academic English writing.

### Purpose of the Study

This study aims to understand:
- How Vietnamese university students use GenAI tools for academic English writing
- What benefits students perceive from using these tools
- What concerns students have about plagiarism and academic integrity when using GenAI

### What You Will Be Asked to Do

- Complete an online questionnaire consisting of multiple-choice and Likert-scale questions
- The questionnaire will take approximately **10-15 minutes** to complete
- You may also provide optional open-ended responses

### Voluntary Participation

- Your participation is **completely voluntary**
- You may withdraw from the study at any time without penalty
- You may skip any question you do not wish to answer
- Your decision to participate or not will **not affect** your grades or academic standing

### Confidentiality & Anonymity

- This survey is **completely anonymous** — no names, student IDs, or email addresses are collected
- Your responses **cannot be linked** to your identity
- All data will be reported in **aggregate form** only
- Raw data will be stored securely and accessible only to the research team
- Data will be retained for a period of **3 years** after the study is completed, then securely destroyed

### Risks and Benefits

- **Risks:** There are no known risks associated with this study. Some questions ask about academic practices, but your anonymity is fully protected.
- **Benefits:** Your participation will contribute to understanding how GenAI tools are used in Vietnamese higher education and may help inform institutional policies and teaching practices.
