This question tests self-awareness and growth mindset. The interviewer knows you have weaknesses — they want to see if you're honest about them and actively working to improve.

## Examples

**Input:** "What is your biggest weakness?"
**Output:** An honest answer showing self-awareness and a concrete improvement plan.

## Solution

```text
Framework: Honest Weakness → Impact → Active Improvement

EXAMPLE 1 (Over-engineering):
"I tend to over-engineer solutions. Early in my career, I'd build
abstractions for hypothetical future requirements instead of solving
the immediate problem. On one project, I spent a week building a
plugin system that we never actually needed.

I've gotten much better by adopting a 'three strikes' rule — I don't
abstract something until I see the same pattern three times. I also
ask myself 'what's the simplest thing that could work?' before
starting any implementation. My last two projects shipped ahead of
schedule because of this shift."

EXAMPLE 2 (Public speaking):
"I'm not a natural public speaker. Leading team demos and presenting
architecture decisions used to make me anxious, and I'd rush through
important details.

I've been actively working on this — I volunteered to present at our
monthly tech talks, joined a local meetup as a speaker, and started
recording practice runs. My last architecture review got positive
feedback specifically on clarity, which was encouraging."
```

## Explanation

**Good weaknesses to mention:**
- Over-engineering / perfectionism (with concrete examples)
- Public speaking or presentation skills
- Difficulty estimating timelines
- Tendency to take on too much work
- Struggles with delegation

**Never say:**
- "I'm a perfectionist" (without real substance — it's a cliché)
- "I work too hard" (transparent humble brag)
- "I don't have any weaknesses" (lack of self-awareness)
- Something core to the job ("I don't like writing code")

**Structure tips:**
- Be genuinely honest — interviewers spot fake answers immediately
- Show the **impact** the weakness had (proves self-awareness)
- Spend most time on the **improvement steps** (shows growth mindset)
- Use past tense for the problem, present tense for the improvement
