**When should you choose microservices over a monolith, and what are the real trade-offs?**

This is one of the most debated architecture questions. The answer is more nuanced than "microservices good, monolith bad."

MICROSERVICES CHALLENGES
в•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җв•җ

1. DISTRIBUTED SYSTEM COMPLEXITY
   - Network failures between services
   - Partial failures (Service A succeeds, Service B fails)
   - Distributed debugging requires tracing (OpenTelemetry)

2. DATA CONSISTENCY
   - No cross-service ACID transactions
   - Eventual consistency via events/sagas
   - Saga pattern for multi-service workflows:
     Order Created вҶ’ Payment Charged вҶ’ Inventory Reserved
     If inventory fails вҶ’ compensate: refund payment, cancel order

3. OPERATIONAL OVERHEAD
   - Dozens of services to deploy, monitor, log
   - Service discovery, load balancing, circuit breakers
   - Kubernetes or similar orchestration
   - Much higher infrastructure cost

4. TESTING
   - Integration tests require running multiple services
   - Contract testing between services (Pact)
   - End-to-end tests are slow and flaky

## Explanation

**The pragmatic path:**

1. **Start with a monolith** вҖ” fastest to build, simplest to operate
2. **Organize as modules** вҖ” clear boundaries, interface-based communication
3. **Extract services when needed** вҖ” when scaling, team autonomy, or deployment speed demands it

**Shopify** runs one of the world's largest Ruby monoliths. **Amazon** famously moved to microservices. **Google** uses a massive monorepo. There's no universal answer.

**The key insight:** Microservices solve **organizational** problems (team scaling, independent deployment) more than technical ones. If you have 5 engineers, microservices add complexity with no benefit. If you have 500 engineers, a monolith creates deployment bottlenecks and merge conflicts.

## ELI5

A monolith is like living in a house вҖ” everything's under one roof, easy to manage, but if you want to renovate the kitchen, the whole family is affected. Microservices are like a neighborhood of small houses вҖ” each family manages their own home, can renovate independently, but now you need roads, mail delivery, and power lines between houses. A modular monolith is like a house with separate apartments вҖ” one building, but each family has their own space.
