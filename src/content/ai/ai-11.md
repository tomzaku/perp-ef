**What is Chain-of-Thought prompting and what are advanced reasoning techniques?**

Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting encourages LLMs to break down complex problems into intermediate reasoning steps, significantly improving performance on math, logic, and multi-step tasks.

## Solution

```text
CHAIN-OF-THOUGHT VARIANTS
══════════════════════════

1. ZERO-SHOT CoT
   Simply add "Let's think step by step" to the prompt.

   Q: A store had 150 apples. They sold 30% on Monday
      and 25% of the remainder on Tuesday. How many are left?
   A: Let's think step by step.
      1. Monday: sold 30% of 150 = 45. Remaining: 150 - 45 = 105
      2. Tuesday: sold 25% of 105 = 26.25 ≈ 26. Remaining: 105 - 26 = 79
      Answer: 79 apples

2. FEW-SHOT CoT
   Provide examples with explicit reasoning chains before the task.

3. SELF-CONSISTENCY
   Generate multiple CoT paths (temperature > 0), take majority vote.
   Path 1: ... → Answer: 79
   Path 2: ... → Answer: 79
   Path 3: ... → Answer: 80
   Majority vote: 79 ✓

4. TREE OF THOUGHT (ToT)
   Explore multiple reasoning branches, evaluate, continue best paths.
   Problem
   ├── Approach A
   │   ├── Step A1 → Dead end (score: 0.2)
   │   └── Step A2 → Promising (score: 0.8) → Continue...
   └── Approach B
       ├── Step B1 → Promising (score: 0.7) → Continue...
       └── Step B2 → Dead end (score: 0.1)

5. ReAct (Reason + Act)
   Interleave reasoning with tool use:
   Thought: I need to find the population of Tokyo
   Action: search("Tokyo population 2024")
   Observation: Tokyo has ~14 million people
   Thought: Now I can compare with New York...


OTHER ADVANCED TECHNIQUES
═════════════════════════

- Least-to-Most: Decompose complex problem into subproblems, solve in order
- Directional Stimulus: Give hints/keywords to guide reasoning direction
- Meta-Prompting: Ask model to write a better prompt, then use it


CHOOSING A TECHNIQUE
════════════════════

Technique          │ Best For                    │ Overhead
───────────────────┼─────────────────────────────┼──────────────
Zero-shot CoT      │ Simple reasoning tasks      │ Minimal
Few-shot CoT       │ Domain-specific reasoning   │ Moderate
Self-Consistency   │ High-stakes accuracy        │ High (N gens)
Tree of Thought    │ Complex planning/search     │ Very high
ReAct              │ Tasks needing external data  │ Moderate + tools
```

## Explanation

The breakthrough insight behind CoT is that LLMs perform better when they **"show their work."** This is because generating intermediate tokens provides the model with additional computation and context for each subsequent step.

Without CoT, the model must jump directly from question to answer in a single forward pass, which is insufficient for complex reasoning. With CoT, each step builds on the previous one, creating a chain of logical deductions.

**When to use CoT:** Any task involving math, logic, multi-step reasoning, or complex decision-making. For simple factual questions, CoT adds unnecessary tokens and cost.

## ELI5

When your math teacher says "show your work," you actually get more answers right because each step helps you figure out the next step. Chain-of-Thought is the same idea for AI — when you ask it to think step by step, it gets much better answers because it can build on each small step instead of trying to jump straight to the final answer.
