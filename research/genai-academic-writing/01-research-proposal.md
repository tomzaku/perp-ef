# Research Proposal

## Title

University Students' Perceptions of Using Generative AI in Academic English Writing: Usage, Benefits, and Plagiarism Concerns — A Case Study from a Vietnamese University

## 1. Introduction & Background

Generative AI (GenAI) tools — particularly ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Microsoft Copilot — have rapidly transformed the landscape of academic writing. Since the public release of ChatGPT in November 2022, university students worldwide have integrated these tools into their academic workflows, including brainstorming, drafting, grammar correction, paraphrasing, and translation.

In the Vietnamese higher education context, English academic writing presents unique challenges. Many students are non-native English speakers who struggle with grammar, vocabulary, coherence, and academic conventions. GenAI tools offer the potential to bridge these gaps, but they also raise significant concerns around plagiarism, academic integrity, and over-reliance on technology.

Despite growing global research on GenAI in education, studies focusing specifically on Vietnamese university students' perceptions remain limited. This study aims to fill that gap by investigating how Vietnamese university students perceive and use GenAI tools for academic English writing, what benefits they experience, and how they view plagiarism-related concerns.

## 2. Research Objectives

1. To investigate the extent and patterns of GenAI usage among Vietnamese university students for academic English writing tasks.
2. To identify the perceived benefits of using GenAI tools in academic English writing.
3. To examine students' awareness and concerns regarding plagiarism when using GenAI tools.
4. To explore whether demographic variables (year of study, English proficiency level, academic discipline) influence students' perceptions of GenAI usage, benefits, and plagiarism concerns.

## 3. Research Questions

- **RQ1:** To what extent do Vietnamese university students use GenAI tools for academic English writing?
- **RQ2:** What are the perceived benefits of using GenAI tools for academic English writing among Vietnamese university students?
- **RQ3:** What are the plagiarism-related concerns Vietnamese university students have regarding the use of GenAI in academic writing?
- **RQ4:** Are there significant differences in GenAI usage patterns, perceived benefits, and plagiarism concerns based on students' demographic characteristics (year of study, English proficiency, academic discipline)?

## 4. Research Design

**Methodology:** Quantitative — Cross-sectional survey design

**Rationale:** A quantitative approach is chosen due to time constraints and the need to collect data from a large sample efficiently. A structured questionnaire enables standardized data collection, statistical analysis, and generalizability of findings.

**Instrument:** Self-administered online questionnaire (Google Forms or similar platform)

**Sampling:**
- **Population:** Undergraduate students at a Vietnamese university who have completed at least one English academic writing course
- **Sampling method:** Convenience sampling combined with stratified distribution across faculties
- **Target sample size:** 200-300 respondents (minimum 200 for adequate statistical power)

## 5. Theoretical Framework

This study draws on:

1. **Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)** (Davis, 1989) — To understand perceived usefulness and perceived ease of use of GenAI tools
2. **Self-Determination Theory (SDT)** (Deci & Ryan, 1985) — To examine intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivations for GenAI adoption
3. **Academic Integrity Framework** — To contextualize plagiarism concerns within institutional and cultural norms

## 6. Data Analysis Plan

| Analysis | Purpose | Tool |
|----------|---------|------|
| Descriptive statistics (mean, SD, frequency) | Describe usage patterns, benefits, concerns | SPSS / Excel |
| Cronbach's alpha | Measure internal consistency of scales | SPSS |
| Independent samples t-test | Compare two groups (e.g., junior vs. senior) | SPSS |
| One-way ANOVA | Compare 3+ groups (e.g., across disciplines) | SPSS |
| Pearson correlation | Examine relationships between variables | SPSS |
| Multiple regression (optional) | Predict plagiarism concerns from other variables | SPSS |

## 7. Ethical Considerations

- Informed consent obtained before survey participation
- Voluntary participation with right to withdraw at any time
- Anonymity guaranteed — no personally identifiable information collected
- Data stored securely and used solely for research purposes
- Institutional ethics review/approval obtained prior to data collection

## 8. Significance of the Study

This study contributes to:
- The limited body of research on GenAI use in Vietnamese higher education
- Understanding how non-native English speakers leverage AI for academic writing
- Informing institutional policies on GenAI usage and academic integrity
- Providing practical recommendations for educators designing writing curricula that integrate or address GenAI tools

## 9. Limitations

- Convenience sampling limits generalizability
- Self-reported data may be subject to social desirability bias
- Cross-sectional design captures perceptions at one point in time
- Single-institution case study may not represent all Vietnamese universities
- Quantitative-only approach may miss nuanced perspectives (mitigated by open-ended optional questions)
