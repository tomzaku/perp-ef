**How do multi-agent systems work and what are the architectural patterns?**

Multi-agent systems decompose complex tasks across multiple specialized AI agents that collaborate, each with their own role, tools, and instructions.

## Explanation

Multi-agent systems apply the software engineering principle of **modularity** to AI. Just as microservices break a monolith into focused services, multi-agent architectures break complex AI tasks into specialized agents.

The tradeoff is the same too: better separation of concerns and scalability, but increased complexity in coordination and communication.

**When to use multi-agent vs single agent:**
- **Single agent**: Task is well-defined, linear, doesn't need diverse tools
- **Multi-agent**: Complex tasks requiring different expertise, parallel subtasks, or adversarial validation

## ELI5

Imagine a team project at school. Instead of one person doing everything, you have a researcher who finds information, a writer who drafts the report, and an editor who checks for mistakes. Each person is really good at their job, and a team leader makes sure they work together smoothly. Multi-agent AI works the same way вҖ” different AI "team members" each handle what they're best at.
