The interviewer wants a concise professional summary — not your life story. This is your chance to frame the conversation and highlight what makes you a strong candidate.

## Examples

**Input:** "Tell me about yourself."
**Output:** A 2-minute structured response covering your current role, key achievements, and why you're excited about this opportunity.

## Solution

```text
Framework: Present → Past → Future

1. PRESENT: "I'm currently a senior frontend engineer at [Company],
   where I lead the design system team building components used by
   200+ engineers."

2. PAST: "Before that, I spent 3 years at [Previous Company] where
   I migrated a legacy jQuery app to React, reducing load time by 60%.
   I also built the real-time collaboration feature that became a key
   selling point."

3. FUTURE: "I'm excited about [Target Company] because of your focus
   on [specific thing]. My experience with [relevant skill] aligns
   well with the challenges your team is tackling."
```

## Explanation

**Why this works:**
- **Present** establishes credibility and current expertise
- **Past** provides evidence with specific, quantifiable achievements
- **Future** shows genuine interest and creates a natural connection to the role

**Common mistakes:**
- Starting from childhood or college — keep it professional and recent
- Being too vague ("I'm a hard worker") — use concrete examples
- Going over 3 minutes — respect the interviewer's time
- Not connecting your story to the role you're applying for

**Tips:**
- Tailor this for each company — mention something specific about them
- Practice until it feels natural, not rehearsed
- Include numbers when possible (team size, performance gains, user impact)
